    New Drug Review and Recommended PA Criteria¶

    DISCLAIMER ALERT: The Ohio UPDL trumps all recommended criteria presented on this page. These are suggested recommended PA criteria for newly released medications. Once ODM evaluates and determines their own specific criteria, that will take precedent.

    Roctavian (valoctocogene roxaparvovec-rvox) 7/25/23¶

    Roctavian (valoctocogene roxaparvovec-rvox).pdf

    WAC Price per Unit and Cost per Year/Treatment
    One vial (16 x 1013 vg/ 8mL) = $90,625. For a 70 kg patient, the one-time treatment price would be $2,378,906.25
    Suggested Prior Authorization/Utilization Management Criteria
    Approval length: one-time approval
    Indication: Treatment of adults with severe hemophilia A (congenital factor VIII deficiency with factor VIII activity < 1 IU/dL) without pre-existing antibodies to adeno-associated virus serotype 5 detected by an FDA-approved test
    Initial Therapy:
  1. Patient age ≥ 18 years1 AND
  2. Male gender assigned at birth2 AND
  3. Diagnosis of severe congenital hemophilia A (Factor VIII activity level ≤1 IU/dL) AND
  4. Documentation of no detectable antibodies to AAV5 AND
  5. No evidence of liver dysfunction, fibrosis or HIV AND
  6. Documentation of prophylaxis with Factor VIII within the previous year
    		7.
    Additional Considerations:
  7. Perform regular ALT testing to monitor for elevations. Elevated liver enzymes, especially elevated ALT, may indicate immune-mediated hepatotoxicity and may be associated with decline in factor VIII activity
    		8.

    Filspari (sparsentan) 3/14/23¶

    Filspari (sparsentan) pdf

    WAC Price per Unit and Cost per Year/Treatment
    1 tablet/ 30 days (for both 200 mg and 400mg) $9,900/ month or $118,000 annually
    Suggested Prior Authorization/Utilization Management Criteria
    Approval length: Initial- 6 months; Continuation of therapy- 6 months
    Indication: Reduce proteinuria in adults with primary immunoglobulin A nephropathy (IgAN) at risk of rapid disease progression, generally a urine protein-to-creatinine ratio (UPCR) ≥1.5 g/g
    Initial Therapy:
  52. Prescribed by or in consultation with a nephrologist or a related specialist AND
  53. Diagnosis of IgAN as confirmed by biopsy AND
  54. Evidence of proteinuria ≥1 g/day and eGFR ≥ 30 mL/ min AND
  55. Trial and failure of an ACE Inhibitor or ARB (90/120 days w/ UPCR ≥1 g/day) or documented contraindication/intolerance to therapy
    		56.
    Continuation of Therapy:
  56. Documentation of UPCR ≤ 1 g/day
  57. eGFR ≥ 30 mL/ mine
    		58.
    Additional Considerations:
  58. Filspari is only available through a REMS program which requires pregnancy testing before, during, and after treatment.
  59. Patients who can become pregnant must use effective contraception prior to initiation of treatment, during treatment, and for one month after discontinuation of treatment.
  60. Measure liver aminotransferases and total bilirubin prior to initiation of treatment and ALT and AST monthly for 12 months, then every 3 months during treatment.
  61. Prior to initiating treatment with Filspari, discontinue use of renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS) inhibitors, endothelin receptor antagonists (ERAs), or aliskiren.
    		62.

    Leqembi (lecanemab-irmb) 1/24/23¶

    Leqembi (lecanemab-irmb) pdf

    WAC Price per Unit and Cost per Year/Treatment
    Annual WAC price estimated for 70 kg patient (dosed 10 mg/kg every two weeks) is $23,184.00.
    Suggested Prior Authorization/Utilization Management Criteria
    Approval length: Initial- 6 months; Continuation of therapy- 6 months
    Indication: Treatment of Alzheimer’s disease (AD). Treatment with Leqembi should be initiated in patients with mild cognitive impairment (MCI) or mild dementia stage of disease. There are no safety or effectiveness data on initiating treatment at earlier or later stages of the disease than were studied. Confirm the presence of amyloid beta pathology prior to initiating treatment.
    Initial Therapy:
  62. Age 50 years or older AND
  63. Prescribed by or in consultation with a neurologist, geriatrician, or other specialist in the treated disease state AND
  64. Documentation of mild dementia or mild cognitive impairment associated with Alzheimer’s disease via MMSE, CDR-SB or ADAS-Cog 14 score AND
  65. Documentation of positive amyloid beta pathology on recent PET scan AND
  66. Documentation of recent brain MRI (within one year) to evaluate for pre-existing Amyloid
    		67.
    Related Imaging Abnormalities (ARIA) AND
  67. Patient must not be prescribed anticoagulant medications
    		68.
    Continuation of Therapy:
  68. Documentation of follow-up MRIs to evaluate for ARIA-E, ARIA-H, and other structural changes prior to the 5th, 7th, and 14th infusions, then at least once annually AND
  69. Documentation of current disease severity as demonstrated by current MMSE score, CDR-SB or ADAS-COG score
    		70.
    Additional Considerations:
  70. If radiographically observed ARIA occurs, treatment recommendations are based on type, severity, and presence of symptoms
  71. Consider testing for ApoE ε4 status to inform the risk of developing ARIA when deciding to initiate treatment with Leqembi
  72. Evaluate patients for evidence of established cardiovascular disease and discuss increased risk of ARIA and complications with Leqembi use in those with positive histories
  73. Because intracerebral hemorrhages greater than 1 cm in diameter have been observed in patients taking Leqembi, additional caution should be exercised when considering the administration of antithrombotics or a thrombolytic agent (e.g., tissue plasminogen activator) to a patient already being treated with Leqembi
  74. Discontinue Leqembi when patient has progressed to moderate AD
    		75.

    Hemgenix® (etranacogene dezaparvovec-drlb) 1/3/23¶

    ***Carve Out Medication Process

    Hemgenix® (etranacogene dezaparvovec-drlb) pdf

    WAC Price per Unit and Cost per Year/Treatment
    $3.5 million for a one-time infusion
    Suggested Prior Authorization/Utilization Management Criteria
    Approval length: 3 months, one infusion per lifetime
    Indication: Treatment of hemophilia B (congenital factor IX [FIX] deficiency) in adults with:
  75. Current or history of life-threatening hemorrhage, OR
  76. Repeated, serious spontaneous bleeding episodes, OR
  77. Current use of FIX prophylaxis therapy
    		78.
    Quantity limit: 2 x 10 genome copies per kg of body weight
    Initial Therapy:
  78. Prescribed by, or in consultation with, a hematologist
  79. Male gender assigned at birth and aged >18 years
  80. Documented diagnosis of hemophilia B based on a factor assay to demonstrate deficiency of FIX AND
    • Documentation of moderate disease (FIX level 1-5IU/dL, or 1%-5% of normal) OR
    • Documentation of severe disease (FIX level <1 IU/dL, or <1% of normal)
  81. Current use of FIX prophylaxis for > 2 months with greater than 150 previous exposure days of treatment with FIX protein OR
    • Have current or historical life-threatening hemorrhage OR
    • Have repeated, serious spontaneous bleeding episodes
  82. Delivery of gene therapy with Hemgenix is done by or in consultation with a HTC
    		83.
    Hemgenix will NOT be covered when ANY the following are present::
  83. History of FIX inhibitors or a positive inhibitor screen conducted within the past 12 months (confirmed as 0.6 BU/mL [Bethesda Unit] or greater by chromogenic Bethesda Assay or other immunologic test)
  84. Documentation of current disease severity as demonstrated by current MMSE score, CDR-SB or ADAS-COG score
  85. Previous gene therapy treatment
  86. Active hepatitis B infection, hepatitis C infection, or uncontrolled HIV infection
  87. History of hepatitis B or C exposure currently controlled by antiviral therapy
  88. Evidence of advanced liver fibrosis (screening laboratory values [i.e., ALT, AST, bilirubin, ALP, creatinine] > 2 times upper limits of normal)
    		89.

    Tzield (teplizumab-mzwv) 12/13/22¶

    Tzield (teplizumab-mzwv) pdf

    ** See Clinical Team Suggestion

    WAC Price per Unit and Cost per Year/Treatment
    $6,925.00 per mL; WAC for one 14-day course of therapy with Tzield is estimated at $193,900 using the BSA of a typical 13-year-old (1.33 m2).
    Suggested Prior Authorization/Utilization Management Criteria
    Approval length: 1-time therapy (14 days)
    Indication: To delay the onset of Stage 3 type 1 diabetes (T1D) in adults and pediatric patients aged 8 years and older with Stage 2 T1D
    Initial Therapy:
  107. Prescribed by or in consultation with an endocrinologist AND
  108. Patient aged 8 years of age and older AND
  109. Diagnosis of Stage 2 T1D by documentation of ALL the following:
    • Presence of ≥ 2 autoantibodies (e.g., GAD65, IAA, IA-2A, ICA, or ZnT8)
    • Evidence of dysglycemia (e.g., fasting glucose (FG) level of 110 to 125 mg/dL (6.1 to 6.9 mmol/L), a 2-hour postprandial plasma glucose level of at least 140 mg/dL (7.8 mmol/L) and less than 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L))
    Tzield will NOT be administered if any of the following is present:
  110. Active and/or chronic infection
  111. Severe lymphopenia (<500 cells per mcL) noted on baseline laboratory assessment
  112. Elevated LFTs on baseline laboratory assessment
  113. Diagnosis of T2D
  114. Diagnosis of Stage 3 T1D
    115. Additional Considerations:
  115. Prior to initiating Tzield, obtain a complete blood count and liver enzyme tests. Use of Tzield is not recommended in patients with certain laboratory abnormalities (i.e., 3x upper limit of normal ALT and/or AST)
  116. Administer all age-appropriate vaccinations prior to starting Tzield
  117. Duration of approval should not exceed 3 months to prevent patients receiving Tzield after progressing to Stage 3 TID due to lapse between approval and administration of drug.
